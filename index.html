Treatment for Common Conditions

Critical care is the specialized care given to patients suffering from life-threatening conditions and requires immediate intensive treatment with close monitoring of the vital parameters. It is also known as intensive care and is usually given in an intensive care unit (ICU) by specially trained health care providers. Monitor, intravenous tubes, feeding tubes, life support systems, breathing machines and other equipments are important requirements in a ICU.

Thoracoscopy, also called Pleuroscopy, it is a medical procedure in which a flexible viewing tube called thoracoscope or the pleuroscope is inserted into the patients chest . When the thoracoscope is inserted via the chest, 1 to 2 small cuts (incisions) are made on the side of the chest between the ribs and through the chest wall. The instrument is inserted through one of the incisions into the chest cavity. In case of the procedure is used to remove small lung tumours, the other incisions are used to put surgical instruments into the chest. General anesthesia or conscious sedation is given to the patient in order to avoid discomfort to the patient.

EBUS bronchoscopy (endobronchial ultrasound) is used to diagnose different lung disorders such as infection, cancer, or inflammation. EBUS bronchoscopy is performed by a pulmonologist using a flexible tube that passes through your mouth and enters the trachea and lungs. Dr. Arda Kiani performs this procedure in the best hospitals in Tehran, Iran.

EBUS allows doctors to perform a technique called transbronchial needle aspiration (TBNA) to get fluid or tissue samples from lymph nodes or lungs without surgery. The samples can be used to diagnose and determine the stage of pulmonary cancer, diagnose infections, and detect inflammatory diseases affecting the lungs including sarcoidosis and cancers like lymphoma.

Pulmonary balloon dilatation is a cardiac catheterization procedure that treats pulmonary stenosis by stretching open a narrowed valve in the heart.A catheter with a deflated balloon at the tip is inserted into a blood vessel in the groin and guided to the heart. A bronchoscope is used to examine the area. A guidewire is placed into the narrowed area. The balloon is passed over the guidewire to the narrowed valve. The balloon is inflated to stretch the valve leaflets and relieve narrowing. The balloon is deflated and the catheters are removed. The incision in the groin is closed and a dressing is applied.

Balloon dilatation can be performed at any age, including in neonates and infants. It can sometimes be a life-saving emergency procedure.

Lung surgery can be called a thoracotomy, video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS), or robotic surgery. A thoracotomy is a type of open surgery that involves making a cut between the ribs to access the lungs.

Thoracotomy

A surgeon makes a cut between the ribs to access the lungs. The surgeon may also remove a rib or separate the ribs. The lung on the side of the cut is deflated to make it easier to operate on.

Lobectomy

A lobe of the lung is removed if there is a problem in only part of the lung. The remaining healthy lung tissue can function normally.

Wedge resection

A small wedge-shaped piece of lung is removed, including a tumor and some healthy tissue around it.

An airway stent procedure is a procedure to insert a hollow, tube-shaped device into a patient's airway to help them breathe.The patient is put under general anesthesia and a breathing tube is inserted. A flexible tube called a bronchoscope is inserted through the patient's nose or mouth and down the throat.A guidewire is threaded through the airway to position the stent, which is then inserted into the bronchoscope and expanded to open the airway.

The procedure is usually performed as an outpatient or day case procedure. Patients typically stay overnight in the hospital and are discharged after a consultation with the oncology and thoracic teams

Asthma

Asthma is a chronic respiratory illness. It involves inflammation of the bronchial tubes carrying oxygen in and carbon dioxide out of the lungs. This inflammation makes the person highly sensitive to triggers such as smoke, dust and pollens.

Bronchitis

Bronchitis is the inflammation of the bronchi, the airways that branch out from the trachea (wind pipe) into the lungs. The inflammation can be caused by viruses, bacteria and other particles which irritate the bronchi.

COPD

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease is a group of lung conditions that make airflow in and out of the lungs difficult due to narrowing of the airways. In COPD, the bronchi and alveolar lose their shape and elasticity.
